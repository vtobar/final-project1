Analysis

The information presented in the NY Data Section allows researchers to ask even more questions about suicide. In this section, we analyze whether there is a relationship between suicides, violent crimes, and income distribution in New York by county. More specifically, we merged these databases by county, to address relevant questions such as:

i. Is there a correlation between “violent” counties and the percentage of suicides?

ii. Are suicides more probable in poor or rich counties?

iii. What are the demographics in counties where suicides are more common?

iv. How have suicide rates changed over the years?

v. If suicide rates present a relevant correlation with certain variables i.e. income, group age, ethnicity, are policy makers addressing these concerns?
